Season's Greetings from San Diego

I have to start by saying that 2019 has not been one of my favorite years. In October, we lost my dear Mum who passed peacefully at the grand old age of 94. She had kept up her usual good spirits and remarkable memory even after reduced mobility caused her to need full-time care. Lisa and I were thrilled that we got to visit her at Easter (photos right and below) and take her for a walk by the sea and into town to do some shopping. I was glad to be able to go back to England for her funeral, and was so warmed by the fond memories shared by those who were able to attend. I even got to meet a first cousin for the first time.

 

Eric and I have spent the year in divorce proceedings, which are unfortunately still ongoing. My attorney is confident they will soon be concluded, after which I shall be returning to my maiden name of Talbot. Moving forward, I hope to be able to continue to live in the same house in San Diego, and I will have plenty of space for visitors – hint, hint.

I continue to work at The League of Amazing Programmers, teaching kids how to write code in Java. My attempt at moving to a job with a more traditional school year did not work out, but I have to admit I do enjoy the low-stress teaching environment with lovely co-workers. Although I have to work evenings, I love having the free morning hours. I just wish I got more holidays for travel and hanging out with visitors (and Lisa, of course).

Missy (the dog) also had a rough year needing 2 surgeries and losing an eye, but she continues to act like a puppy when she isn’t snoozing.

In addition to the two trips to England, I got to travel to two major Irish dance competitions this year. The first was in Vancouver in July, and the second was in Denver in November. I enjoyed seeing Vancouver (for the first time) and even got to catch up with some old friends who moved there years ago. While I enjoyed the trip, I found the grey skies and rainy days overshadowed the beauty of the surroundings. I was glad to get back to sunny San Diego! The Denver competition resulted in my very first competition recall, and a place on the podium wearing a sash! I can’t tell you how much I enjoyed this experience! It only took me 10 years!

Lisa and I were once again able to perform at Comic-Con (photo below). This year we performed a parody of The Muppet Show song. So much fun and we won the prize for "Best Presentation”"! I was very happy that Lisa was home on her summer break and could take part in this with me.

Since I entered two major dance competitions in 2019, running took a back seat to dance this year. However, I was able to complete a couple of half marathons and a few 5Ks. I will never be fast, but it feels good to go out and run by the ocean most weekends. I also belong to a very fun running club that supports all levels of ability.

Wishing you all a very happy holiday season!
