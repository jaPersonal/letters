Season's Greetings from San Diego

This year has been quite a year of change. Lisa graduated from high school in June (left) and most of you probably already know that she was accepted into her first choice university. Lisa is currently at Bristol University in England (right), studying Psychology. It has been a big adjustment for both of us. Me becoming an empty nester and Lisa starting her life as an adult a long way from home. I look forward to our daily chats and love all the photos she sends so I can see her new friends and have a window into her life there. I hope to go over to the UK at Easter to meet everyone in person.

 

Lisa had a wonderful senior year at high school and was awarded the “Performance Artist of the Year” by her music and theatre teachers (below). She also discovered the delights of vintage clothing and found a gorgeous 1950’s party dress for her final prom (right).

I continue to work at The League of Amazing Programmers, teaching kids how to write code in Java. It’s a great job, but I have decided to try to return to a more traditional school so I can travel to see my mum and Lisa for longer periods of time. Mum is now 93 years old and living in a care home. She has had many bouts with illness this year that caused her to lose touch with reality from time to time. She did not at first recognize me when I visited in September, and I want to be able to visit with her more while I still have her.

My favorite pastimes are hanging out with my Irish dance crew -

 and my running club friends (below). Not only do they get me off the couch, they are all great people and a lot of fun to be around.

In November, I got to visit Portland, Oregon for the first time. It was the location of our annual Irish dance competition, so a whole group of us went and had a great time sampling the wines of the region. We were blessed with gorgeous weather and I had a wonderful vacation!

One of the highlights of this year was Comic-Con! Lisa and I joined with a group of Irish dance friends to perform in the masquerade event (below). We presented a mash up of Dr. Who  and Lord of the Dance in a routine called “Timelords of the Dance”. The crowd of thousands loved us and we won many prizes. What a rush!

 

Jackie and Lisa wish you all a very happy holiday season!
